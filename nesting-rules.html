The nesting rules of html

The elements that are "inside" of the other elements are known as "nested elements," and they are essential to building any webpage today. As they are essential, they have fundamental rules which are needed to apply. Not caring those rules has bad impacts on your work because if your HTML is incorrectly nested, it won't be as accessible to screen readers and older browsers â€” and it could even completely break the visual appearance of a page if the browsers cannot figure out how to properly render a page because HTML elements and tags are out of place.

These are the rules, which you need to apply to:

  • All tags must be nested properly.
  • If you insert other tags, close them in reverse order.
  • Indent the child elements.
  • Every start tag needs to be ended.
  • You can't have end tag without start tag.

Don't do this! 

        <nav>
        <ul><li> <a src="url"> email me </li></a>

        <li><a src="url" older post </li></a>
        </nav>
